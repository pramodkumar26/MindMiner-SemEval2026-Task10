MindMiner at SemEval-2026 Task 10

Multi-Model Approaches to Conspiracy Detection and Psycholinguistic Marker Extraction

Abstract

Conspiracy narratives on social media often hide in subtle word cues and quiet reasoning patterns, making their detection a challenging task for NLP systems. SemEval-2026 Task 10 PsyCoMark introduces a benchmark pairing binary conspiracy detection with extraction of five psycholinguistic markers: Actor, Action, Effect, Victim, and Evidence. We examine how modern transformer-based models grasp both conspiratorial intent and the deeper reasoning structures behind such narratives, using rehydrated Reddit comments annotated by experts in psychology and linguistics. Our best system reaches 0.80 weighted F1 on conspiracy detection and 0.16 macro F1 on marker extraction, with per-marker F1 ranging from 0.36 (Actor) to 0.00 (Victim).

Actor markers are explicit noun phrases with clear syntactic boundaries. Victim markers are typically implicit pronouns scattered across sentences, giving span-tagging models nothing to anchor to. This is not a capacity problem, it is a structural one.
